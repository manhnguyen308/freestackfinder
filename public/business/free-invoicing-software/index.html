Best Free Invoicing Software in 2026 — Get Paid Without the Monthly Fee

Wave, Zoho Invoice, Invoice Ninja, and Square all have genuinely free invoicing plans. Which tool fits your billing volume — and what each one catches you on.

Published April 13, 2026 · 9 min read FreeStackFinder Team

Quick verdict

For most freelancers, Wave is the best free invoicing solution in 2026 — unlimited invoices, unlimited clients, and a complete accounting layer, all at zero cost forever. If you want a cleaner client portal and automated payment reminders with a small, stable client roster, Zoho Invoice is the more polished choice (5-client cap applies). For project-based billing with built-in time tracking, Invoice Ninja handles 20 clients free and is the strongest option for agencies and consultants. Free invoicing tools make paid software unnecessary for solo freelancers — FreshBooks, QuickBooks, and Xero add complexity that most people billing 1–20 clients a month simply do not need.

Why freelancers pay for invoicing software they don’t need

FreshBooks starts at $19/month. QuickBooks Simple Start is $35/month. For a freelancer sending ten invoices a month and chasing two or three payments, that is $228–$420 per year for a billing workflow that Wave handles for nothing. The paid-tier features — payroll, inventory, advanced reporting, bank sync at scale — are genuinely useful for businesses with employees and complex accounts. They are overkill for someone who invoices clients and tracks expenses.

The deeper issue is that invoicing software companies design their pricing to capture users at the growth moment when adding a tool feels easier than finding a free alternative. Once a freelancer is billing through FreshBooks, switching feels like work. The alternatives below require real setup, but none of them require a credit card — and Wave has specifically built its business model to keep invoicing free permanently by monetising on payment processing instead.

The best free invoicing software in 2026

1. Wave — best overall free invoicing for freelancers

What it is: A free cloud-based accounting, invoicing, and payment platform built specifically for freelancers and solo business owners. Wave makes money from payment processing fees, not subscriptions.

Free plan includes:

  • Unlimited invoices, estimates, and recurring invoices
  • Unlimited clients
  • Customisable invoice templates with your logo and branding
  • Automatic payment reminders
  • Receipt scanning and expense tracking
  • Full double-entry accounting (profit/loss and balance sheet)
  • Bank and credit card connection
  • Multi-currency invoicing

What the free plan is missing:

  • Payment processing costs 2.9% + $0.60 per card transaction (not free, but per-transaction — no subscription)
  • Payroll is a paid add-on (not needed for most solo freelancers)
  • Phone support requires paid plan
  • No built-in time tracking

Who it’s best for: Freelancers, consultants, and solo operators who want a complete billing and accounting system at zero recurring cost. Wave covers the full workflow from estimate to paid invoice to financial report without any monthly fee.

Why it stands out: Wave is genuinely free — not a free trial, not a crippled version of a paid product. It is a real accounting and invoicing platform that has operated on the payment-processing revenue model since its founding. The double-entry accounting means your accountant can work with Wave’s export at tax time without translation. For freelancers who do not need payroll or inventory, the free tier covers everything a paid tool would.

2. Zoho Invoice — best free invoicing with a client portal

What it is: Part of the Zoho ecosystem, Zoho Invoice is a dedicated invoicing tool (separate from the full Zoho Books accounting product) with a permanently free tier.

Free plan includes:

  • Up to 5 clients
  • 1,000 invoices per year
  • Multi-currency invoicing
  • Customisable invoice templates
  • Automated payment reminders
  • Client portal — clients can view invoice history and pay online
  • Time tracking and project-based billing
  • Expense tracking
  • Integration with Zoho CRM and Zoho Mail

What the free plan is missing:

  • 5-client cap is the main constraint — once your roster grows, you hit the ceiling
  • 1,000 invoices/year (83/month) is sufficient for most solo freelancers but restrictive for agencies
  • Full accounting features require Zoho Books (separate product, paid)

Who it’s best for: Freelancers with a small, stable client base who want a more polished invoicing experience than Wave, or those already using Zoho CRM or Zoho Mail who want everything in one ecosystem.

Why it stands out: Zoho Invoice’s client portal is more professional than Wave’s for client-facing billing. Clients receive a branded portal where they can view invoice history, download documents, and pay online — this matters when clients have accounts payable processes that require documentation. The automated payment reminders are configurable per-client and reduce the awkward follow-up conversation for late payments. Within the 5-client limit, the feature set is comparable to paid invoicing tools.

Try Zoho Invoice free →

3. Invoice Ninja — best free invoicing for project and time-based billing

What it is: An open-source invoicing platform with a free hosted tier (up to 20 clients) and a self-hosting option for unlimited use. Strong on project-based and time-tracked billing.

Free plan includes:

  • Up to 20 clients on the hosted plan (self-hosted: unlimited)
  • Unlimited invoices, quotes, and proposals
  • Time tracking linked directly to invoices
  • Expense tracking
  • Project billing (bill by project, not just by amount)
  • Recurring invoices
  • Client portal
  • Multiple payment gateway integrations (Stripe, PayPal, WePay, and others)

What the free plan is missing:

  • 20-client limit on the hosted plan is a hard ceiling
  • Some template customisation requires the paid tier
  • Self-hosted option requires a VPS and technical setup (but unlocks everything free)

Who it’s best for: Agencies, consultants, and developers who bill by the hour or by project. The time-tracking-to-invoice workflow is cleaner than Wave’s for project-based billing.

Why it stands out: Invoice Ninja’s time tracking integration is the feature that separates it from Wave and Zoho Invoice. If your invoices are built from tracked hours rather than fixed amounts, logging time inside Invoice Ninja and converting it directly to a line-item invoice is meaningfully faster than exporting from a separate time tracker and manually building an invoice. The open-source self-hosted version is unlimited and free — for technical users comfortable with a VPS, this is the strongest option in this list.

Try Invoice Ninja free →

4. Square Invoices — best for service businesses taking card payments

What it is: Square’s invoicing product, part of the broader Square payments ecosystem. Free plan covers invoices, estimates, and contracts with no monthly subscription.

Free plan includes:

  • Unlimited invoices, estimates, and contracts
  • Automated payment reminders
  • Milestone payments (split a project invoice into 2–3 payments)
  • Customer directory
  • Square POS integration
  • Digital contracts with e-signature
  • Recurring invoices

What the free plan is missing:

  • Payment processing is 3.3% + $0.30 per invoice payment (standard card rate)
  • No accounting features — invoicing only
  • No time tracking
  • Limited multi-currency support

Who it’s best for: Service businesses — cleaners, contractors, photographers, tutors — who already use or would benefit from Square’s payment ecosystem. The POS integration means in-person and remote card payments run through the same system.

Why it stands out: Square Invoices’ digital contract and e-signature feature is its differentiator. Sending a signed contract and an invoice in a single workflow removes friction from the proposal-to-payment process for service businesses that need signed agreements before starting work. The milestone payments feature is useful for longer projects where clients prefer staged billing. Square’s recognisable brand also reduces payment friction with individual clients compared to less-familiar platforms.

Try Square Invoices free →

5. PayPal Invoicing — best for clients who prefer PayPal

What it is: PayPal’s built-in invoicing feature, available to any PayPal Business account at no subscription cost.

Free plan includes:

  • Unlimited invoices to unlimited clients
  • Customisable invoice templates
  • Payment tracking and history
  • Partial payment support
  • QR code payment links
  • Automatic payment reminders

What the free plan is missing:

  • Transaction fee: 3.49% + $0.49 per payment — higher than Square or Stripe
  • No accounting features
  • No project or time-based billing
  • Limited template customisation compared to Wave or Zoho Invoice

Who it’s best for: Freelancers whose clients already use or prefer PayPal, particularly international clients in regions where PayPal is the dominant payment method, and anyone who wants zero setup friction.

Why it stands out: PayPal’s primary advantage is client trust. Many clients — especially individuals and small businesses — are more comfortable paying a PayPal invoice than entering card details into an unfamiliar system. If reducing payment friction matters more than polished invoice branding, PayPal’s name recognition works in your favour. The invoicing feature is basic, but it gets money from client to bank account reliably with minimal friction on both sides.

Try PayPal Invoicing free →

6. Stripe Invoicing — best for technical and developer-led businesses

What it is: Stripe’s hosted invoicing product, built for developers and technical businesses already using Stripe for payments.

Free plan includes:

  • Unlimited manual invoices (one-off billing)
  • 25 free automated invoice emails per month (then 0.4% per automated invoice)
  • PDF invoices with branding
  • Customer portal
  • Tax rate management
  • Multi-currency support
  • Full API access for custom billing workflows

What the free plan is missing:

  • 0.4% fee applies to automated subscription billing (not manual one-off invoices)
  • No expense tracking or accounting
  • Interface assumes familiarity with Stripe’s ecosystem
  • Not designed for non-developers

Who it’s best for: Developers, SaaS businesses, and technical consultants already on Stripe who want invoice and subscription billing in the same platform — without switching to a separate invoicing tool.

Why it stands out: Stripe Invoicing is the only option here with a real API for custom billing workflows. If you need programmatic invoice generation — billing clients based on usage, integrating invoices into your own product dashboard, or creating custom payment schedules — Stripe’s developer-first design handles this natively. For non-technical users, Wave or Zoho Invoice are easier to use without the Stripe context.

When free invoicing is enough — and when to pay

Free invoicing covers the vast majority of solo freelancer needs. You do not need paid software if you have fewer than 20 regular clients, your business is service-based without inventory, you file as a sole trader or small LLC, and you do not have employees requiring payroll.

The case for paid invoicing software starts when your business scales past solo operation. FreshBooks’ paid tiers add team collaboration and accountant access. QuickBooks becomes relevant when payroll, inventory, or multi-user access is a genuine requirement. For most freelancers billing 1–20 clients a month, the free options above cover everything today — and for the next three to five years of growth.

Our verdict

Wave is the default free invoicing recommendation: unlimited invoices, unlimited clients, built-in accounting, and a permanently free business model. Zoho Invoice is the upgrade if you want a polished client portal and automated follow-up — just know the 5-client ceiling. Invoice Ninja wins for project-based and hourly billing.
