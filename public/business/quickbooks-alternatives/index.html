Best Free Alternatives to QuickBooks for Freelancers in 2026

QuickBooks charges monthly fees most freelancers don't need to pay. We tested the best free accounting tools — including one that is completely free forever.

Published March 18, 2026 · 9 min read FreeStackFinder Team

Quick verdict

This guide is for freelancers, solopreneurs, and very small businesses who need to track income and expenses, send invoices, and stay on top of their finances — without paying QuickBooks’ monthly fee. For most freelancers, Wave is the best free QuickBooks alternative: it is completely free forever for accounting and invoicing, with no transaction limits and no artificial caps on the core features. If you already use Zoho’s suite of tools, Zoho Books’ free tier is a strong alternative. QuickBooks is worth paying for when you have employees, need payroll processing, or require advanced inventory management — for freelancers working alone, the free tools below cover everything you actually need.

Why freelancers look for a free alternative to QuickBooks

QuickBooks is genuinely powerful software — it handles payroll, inventory, tax preparation, and multi-user access in ways that free tools cannot match. The problem for freelancers is that most of that power is irrelevant to their actual workflow. A freelance designer, consultant, or developer typically needs to do four things: track what money came in, track what money went out, send invoices to clients, and hand something useful to an accountant at tax time. QuickBooks charges a significant monthly fee for a product built around needs that most solo operators will never have.

Several genuinely capable free tools now exist — not free trials, not freemium tools with hard limits, but tools that are actually free for the core accounting workflow. Wave is the most prominent example: it launched as free and remains free for accounting and invoicing because it monetises through payment processing and payroll add-ons rather than a subscription. That is a fundamentally different business model from QuickBooks, and it means the free tier is the actual product rather than a sales funnel.

The trade-off is real but acceptable for most freelancers: free accounting tools have less automation, less polish, and more limited integrations than QuickBooks. For someone billing 5–20 clients per month and tracking a few hundred transactions per year, that trade-off is straightforward to make.

The best free QuickBooks alternatives in 2026

1. Wave — best completely free accounting for freelancers

What it is: A free cloud-based accounting, invoicing, and receipt management platform built specifically for freelancers and very small businesses.

Free plan includes:

  • Unlimited income and expense tracking
  • Unlimited invoicing with customisable professional templates
  • Unlimited bank and credit card connections
  • Receipt scanning via mobile app
  • Financial reports including profit and loss, balance sheet, and cash flow
  • Multi-currency support
  • Completely free — no subscription, no transaction limit, no expiry

What the free plan is missing:

  • Payment processing (accepting credit cards or bank transfers on invoices) has per-transaction fees
  • Payroll is a paid add-on — not relevant for most solo freelancers
  • Bookkeeping and accountant support services are paid extras
  • Some advanced reporting is more limited than QuickBooks

Who it’s best for: Freelancers, consultants, and sole traders who invoice clients regularly and need clean financial records for tax purposes without paying monthly for software.

Why it stands out: Wave’s free tier is not a limited version of a paid product — it is the full accounting product, monetised through optional payment processing rather than a subscription. For a freelancer who invoices clients and tracks expenses, Wave provides everything needed to produce reports an accountant can work with at tax time. The invoice templates are professional, the bank connection sync works reliably, and the mobile receipt scanning app saves significant time at tax season. No other tool in this list matches the depth of Wave’s free accounting features. For the broader tools freelancers need alongside accounting, see our free Slack alternatives for small teams.

2. Zoho Books free tier — best for freelancers already using Zoho tools

What it is: Zoho’s cloud accounting platform with a free tier available for businesses below a certain annual revenue threshold.

Free plan includes:

  • Up to 1,000 invoices per year
  • Bank reconciliation and transaction import
  • Basic expense tracking
  • Client portal for invoice viewing and payment
  • Integration with other Zoho apps including CRM and Projects
  • Automated payment reminders

What the free plan is missing:

  • Free tier is limited to businesses under a revenue threshold — verify current limits for your region
  • Limited to 1 user on the free plan
  • Automated workflows and custom reports require paid plans
  • Some integrations with non-Zoho tools are behind paid tiers

Who it’s best for: Freelancers and small businesses already using Zoho CRM or other Zoho products, and users who want a more structured invoicing workflow than Wave provides.

Why it stands out: Zoho Books sits at a higher level of polish than Wave for invoicing and client management. The client portal and automated payment reminders are more refined, and the integration with Zoho CRM is genuinely useful for anyone managing client relationships alongside billing. The 1,000-invoice annual limit is sufficient for most solo freelancers and the revenue threshold is generous enough that the majority of people reading this article will qualify for the free tier.

Try Zoho Books free →

3. Invoice Ninja free tier — best for hourly billing and project-based work

What it is: An open-source invoicing and billing platform with a generous free tier and a self-hosting option for complete data control.

Free plan includes:

  • Up to 20 clients on the free hosted plan
  • Unlimited invoices, quotes, and credit notes
  • Time tracking and project-based billing
  • Expense tracking
  • Recurring invoices
  • Self-hosting option with no client limits for technical users

What the free plan is missing:

  • 20-client cap on the free hosted plan limits scalability as your business grows
  • Bank sync and reconciliation tools are more limited than Wave or Zoho Books
  • Financial reporting is less comprehensive than dedicated accounting tools
  • Advanced features like purchase orders require paid plans

Who it’s best for: Freelancers who bill by the hour or track time against projects, and technically capable users who want to self-host for unlimited clients.

Why it stands out: Invoice Ninja’s time tracking and project billing features are stronger than any other free tool in this list. For freelancers who bill hourly, the ability to log time and convert it directly into an invoice removes a meaningful manual step. The self-hosting option effectively removes all limits for users comfortable running their own server — unlimited clients, unlimited invoices, complete data ownership, at effectively zero ongoing cost.

Try Invoice Ninja free →

4. GnuCash — best free desktop accounting for offline users

What it is: A free, open-source desktop accounting application that runs locally on your computer with no internet connection or cloud dependency required.

Free plan includes:

  • Full double-entry bookkeeping
  • Income and expense tracking
  • Invoicing and billing
  • Tax reporting features
  • Multi-currency support
  • Available on Windows, macOS, and Linux
  • Completely free and open-source — no subscription, no cloud account

What the free plan is missing:

  • No cloud sync or mobile access — everything is stored locally on your machine
  • Interface is dated compared to modern cloud tools
  • No automatic bank connection — transactions must be imported manually via CSV
  • Steeper learning curve for users unfamiliar with double-entry accounting principles

Who it’s best for: Freelancers who prefer desktop software over cloud tools, privacy-conscious users who do not want financial data stored on third-party servers, and Linux users wanting a fully capable local accounting option.

Why it stands out: GnuCash is the only tool in this list that keeps your financial data entirely on your own computer. For users with genuine privacy concerns about storing income and client data in a third-party cloud service, that is a meaningful advantage that no cloud-based free tool can match. The double-entry bookkeeping system is the same methodology used in professional accounting software — if you learn GnuCash properly, the financial concepts transfer directly to any professional tool. The trade-off is a dated interface and more manual data entry.

Download GnuCash free →

5. FreshBooks free trial — worth knowing about honestly

What it is: A cloud accounting platform popular with freelancers and service-based businesses, with a 30-day free trial.

Free plan includes:

  • 30-day free trial of all paid features — there is no permanent free tier

What the free plan is missing:

  • Everything after the trial — FreshBooks requires a paid subscription to continue

Who it’s best for: Freelancers who want to evaluate a polished, purpose-built accounting tool before committing to a paid plan.

Why it stands out: FreshBooks appears in almost every “free QuickBooks alternative” list — but it is not actually free. We are including it here to be transparent: if you search for free accounting tools and find FreshBooks, the 30-day trial is useful for evaluation but it is not a long-term free solution. FreshBooks is genuinely one of the better-designed accounting tools for freelancers and worth considering if you have outgrown Wave’s capabilities and are ready to pay for something more polished. For a permanently free solution, Wave is the honest answer.

Who should still pay for QuickBooks?

QuickBooks makes the most sense for small businesses that have crossed from solo freelancing into running a small company — with employees, inventory, or multiple users who need access to the same financial records. The payroll processing feature alone is worth the subscription for anyone with even one employee, because the tax compliance requirements around payroll are complex enough that manual handling creates real liability.

Businesses in industries where inventory tracking is central — retail, manufacturing, product-based e-commerce — also need QuickBooks or a comparable paid tool. Wave and Zoho Books free are built around service businesses that sell time and expertise, not physical goods. Trying to track inventory in Wave will create workarounds that cost more in time than the subscription saves in money.

Tax season complexity is another factor. For freelancers working across multiple countries, with complex expense structures, or who need to track VAT or GST across different jurisdictions, the automation and compliance features in QuickBooks’ paid tier save enough accountant time to justify the cost. A good accountant can tell you quickly whether your business complexity warrants the investment.

Our verdict

For the vast majority of freelancers and solo operators, Wave is the only free QuickBooks alternative worth using as a long-term solution — genuinely free forever, covers the full accounting workflow, and produces reports accountants can work with at tax time. If you are already in the Zoho ecosystem, Zoho Books’ free tier is a strong second choice with a more polished invoicing experience. For freelancers who bill by the hour, Invoice Ninja handles project-based billing more cleanly than either alternative. QuickBooks remains the right tool when your business has grown beyond solo operation and payroll, inventory, or multi-user access becomes a genuine requirement — not before.
