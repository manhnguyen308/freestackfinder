Best Free Screen Recording Software in 2026 — No Watermark, No Time Limit

Loom's free plan now limits recordings to 5 minutes. OBS Studio records unlimited, watermark-free, at no cost. Here's how every free screen recorder compares — and which one is right for your use case.

Published April 11, 2026 · 10 min read FreeStackFinder Team

Quick verdict

Loom’s free plan now restricts recordings to 5 minutes and caps storage at 25 videos — which makes it impractical for anything longer than a quick walkthrough. For users who need unlimited recording time and no watermarks, OBS Studio is the best free screen recording software available: it is the same tool used by professional streamers and video producers, completely free, and has no recording limits of any kind. For Windows users who want screenshot annotation alongside screen recording, ShareX is the most feature-complete free option on the platform. For quick, shareable screen recordings with a polished viewer link, Loom Free still works for short content despite its 5-minute limit. For educators recording lessons in Chrome, Screencastify Free is purpose-built for the use case.

Why Loom’s free tier changes drove people to search for alternatives

Loom was the default recommendation for free screen recording for several years — its combination of instant shareable links, automatic cloud hosting, and clean viewer interface made it significantly easier to use than OBS Studio for non-technical users. The free tier was genuinely generous: unlimited recordings, unlimited storage, up to 25 videos.

In 2023, Loom (acquired by Atlassian) tightened the free tier to 25 videos total and 5-minute recording limits per video. For anyone using Loom for product demos, onboarding videos, or training content, this change immediately pushed recordings over the limit.

The alternatives below offer unlimited recording in different ways — OBS Studio with no cloud hosting, ShareX with local file output, and Clipchamp as a simple Windows-native option. For editing recorded footage after capture, see our free video editing software guide for the best free editors that work with OBS output files.

The best free screen recording software in 2026

1. OBS Studio — best free screen recorder for most users

What it is: A free, open-source screen recording and live streaming application used by professional content creators, streamers, and video producers worldwide.

Free plan includes:

  • Unlimited recording length — no time caps
  • No watermarks on output
  • Record screen, webcam, or both simultaneously
  • Multiple scene configurations (switch between layouts mid-recording)
  • Audio mixing — capture desktop audio, microphone, or both independently
  • Output formats: MKV, MP4, MOV, FLV, and more
  • Resolution up to 4K
  • Available on Windows, macOS, and Linux
  • Completely free — no premium tier, no subscription

What it is missing:

  • No built-in cloud hosting or shareable link — recordings save locally, you host them yourself
  • Steeper setup curve than Loom or Screencastify — requires initial configuration of scenes and sources
  • No built-in annotation tools during recording
  • No automatic transcription or AI-generated summaries

Who it’s best for: Anyone who needs unlimited, watermark-free screen recording for tutorials, product demos, course content, or any video that exceeds 5 minutes. Also the right choice for users who want full control over output quality and format.

Why it stands out: OBS Studio is the professional standard for screen recording, not just a free tool that happens to work. The scenes and sources system means you can set up multiple recording configurations — screen only, screen with webcam overlay, presentation mode — and switch between them. The audio mixer lets you independently control levels for desktop audio and microphone. Most users do not need these advanced features, but having them at zero cost means OBS grows with you as your recording needs become more sophisticated.

2. ShareX — best free screen recorder for Windows power users

What it is: A free, open-source screen capture and screen recording tool for Windows with an extensive feature set including annotation, workflow automation, and over 80 upload destinations.

Free plan includes:

  • Unlimited screen recording — no watermarks, no time limits
  • Screenshot capture with instant annotation tools (arrows, text, blur, highlight)
  • Scrolling capture for long web pages
  • GIF recording
  • Optical character recognition (OCR) on screenshots
  • Automatic file upload to Imgur, Dropbox, Google Drive, and 80+ destinations
  • Hotkey customisation for every function
  • Workflow automation — capture, annotate, and upload in one keystroke

What it is missing:

  • Windows-only — no macOS or Linux support
  • Interface is complex — the breadth of features makes it harder to learn than simpler tools
  • No built-in cloud hosting with a shareable viewer link

Who it’s best for: Windows power users who want screen recording combined with screenshot annotation, and anyone who captures screens frequently enough that workflow shortcuts pay off.

Why it stands out: ShareX does more than any other free screen capture tool on Windows — the combination of recording, annotation, OCR, and automated upload covers every screen capture use case in a single application. The automatic upload workflow, where a screenshot is captured, annotated, and uploaded to your chosen host in one action, is a genuine productivity feature once configured. For users who only need basic recording, ShareX is overkill. For users who use screen capture tools daily across multiple contexts, it eliminates the need for several separate tools.

3. Loom Free — best for quick shareable recordings under 5 minutes

What it is: A cloud-based screen recording tool with an automatic shareable link, built-in viewer, and asynchronous video messaging features.

Free plan includes:

  • Recordings up to 5 minutes in length
  • Up to 25 videos in total storage
  • Automatic shareable link generated after recording
  • Clean in-browser viewer with comment and reaction support
  • Screen, camera, or screen-plus-camera recording modes
  • Chrome extension and desktop app available
  • Basic transcript (AI-generated, accuracy varies)

What the free plan is missing:

  • 5-minute recording limit — hard cap, cannot be extended on free
  • 25 video storage limit — oldest videos must be deleted to make room
  • No custom branding or viewer customisation on free
  • Download of recordings requires paid plan on some configurations
  • Drawing tools during recording require paid

Who it’s best for: Teams sending short async video messages — status updates, quick feedback on designs, brief product walkthroughs under 5 minutes. The shareable link workflow is the feature that justifies Loom despite its limits.

Why it stands out: Loom’s shareable link experience is genuinely better than OBS or ShareX for quick communication. A Loom link opens in a browser, plays immediately, and allows the recipient to comment and react without downloading anything. For internal team communication where videos are short and the recipient experience matters, Loom’s 5-minute free tier covers the use case. For anything longer, OBS is the answer.

4. Screencastify — best for educators and Chrome users

What it is: A Chrome extension-based screen recorder designed primarily for educators, with a free tier that allows unlimited recordings with some restrictions.

Free plan includes:

  • Unlimited recordings (limited to 30 minutes each on free)
  • Screen, browser tab, or webcam recording
  • Saves directly to Google Drive
  • Basic annotation during recording (pen tool)
  • Shareable Google Drive link after recording
  • Works entirely in Chrome — no desktop app download required

What the free plan is missing:

  • Watermark on free recordings
  • Advanced editing features require paid
  • No MP4 export on free — recordings stay in Google Drive format
  • Only works in Chrome — no other browser support

Who it’s best for: Teachers and educators who record lessons or feedback videos in Chrome and share them via Google Classroom or Google Drive. The Google Drive integration makes distribution to students seamless.

Why it stands out: Screencastify’s zero-friction entry point — install the Chrome extension, click record, video goes to Google Drive — is genuinely the fastest path from “I need to record this” to “here’s the link.” The watermark on free recordings is the main trade-off. For educators where the viewer is a student rather than a professional client, the watermark is rarely a concern.

5. Clipchamp — best zero-install option for Windows 11 users

What it is: Microsoft’s video creation tool, built into Windows 11, which includes a screen recorder alongside basic video editing capabilities.

Free plan includes:

  • Screen recording with no time limits
  • Webcam recording
  • Basic video editing — cut, trim, text overlay, transitions
  • Export at 1080p on free
  • No watermarks
  • Pre-installed on Windows 11 — no download needed

What the free plan is missing:

  • Limited to basic editing — not a full video editor
  • Premium stock footage and music require paid Microsoft subscription
  • Less capable than OBS for recording quality control
  • Windows 11 only — not available on Windows 10 or macOS

Who it’s best for: Windows 11 users who want a quick, no-install screen recorder for occasional use and basic edits without downloading anything.

Why it stands out: Clipchamp’s main advantage is that it is already on your machine if you use Windows 11. Opening the Start menu, searching Clipchamp, and starting a screen recording takes under 30 seconds with no downloads, no accounts, and no configuration. For occasional recording without the setup investment of OBS, Clipchamp is the zero-friction answer.

OBS Studio setup in 5 minutes

OBS Studio’s interface looks complex on first launch because it is built for live streaming with multiple simultaneous sources. For basic screen recording, the setup is straightforward:

Open OBS. In the Sources panel at the bottom, click the + icon and select Display Capture (to record your whole screen) or Window Capture (to record a specific app). If you want your webcam, add a Video Capture Device source and resize it to sit in the corner of the canvas. In Settings → Output, set Recording Format to MP4 for maximum compatibility. Click Start Recording. When done, click Stop Recording — your file is in the path shown in Settings → Output.

That is the complete setup for 95% of recording use cases. The scenes, transitions, and advanced audio mixing are there when you need them, but are not required for basic use.

Who should pay for a screen recording tool?

Paid screen recording tools — Loom Business ($12.50/user/month), Camtasia ($299.99 one-time), ScreenFlow (Mac, $149 one-time) — add value primarily through built-in video editing, chapter markers, interactive elements, and team management features. For anyone producing professional course content or polished product demos who wants recording and editing in one tool, the paid options are faster than the OBS-plus-editor workflow.

For most users — developers recording bug reproductions, product managers sharing walkthroughs, teachers sending feedback, support teams creating tutorials — OBS Studio’s free unlimited recording covers the full need without cost.

Our verdict

OBS Studio is the correct free screen recorder for any recording that needs to be longer than 5 minutes, of professional quality, or output in a specific format. It has no meaningful limitations on the free tier and is the same tool used by professional video producers. The setup investment is 5–10 minutes. For quick async messages under 5 minutes where the recipient experience matters more than recording length, Loom Free is still useful despite its limits. ShareX is the Windows-only pick for users who need annotation and workflow automation alongside recording. For zero-install simplicity on Windows 11, Clipchamp covers basic needs without any configuration.
