## 30-Day SEO Action Plan

### Week 1 — Technical Foundation
- Keyword research: target "free [category] software" and "best free [tool]", prioritise long-tail first
- Verify Google Analytics and Google Search Console are connected and tracking
- Check site speed and Core Web Vitals in PageSpeed Insights
- Confirm structured data (JSON-LD schema) is present on article pages
- Establish basic internal link structure between silo articles

### Week 2 — Content Foundation
- Publish 2 strong articles from the Early queue
- Ensure each article answers the top People Also Ask questions for its keyword
- Add comparison tables to all existing articles that are missing them
- Add internal links from new articles back to existing cluster articles

### Week 3 — Build Cluster Depth
- Publish 2 more articles
- Review GSC for first impressions data on early articles
- Identify any early-ranking keywords and strengthen those pages with better titles or content
- Add affiliate CTAs to any high-intent pages still missing them

### Weeks 4–8 — Steady Publishing Rhythm
- Maintain 2 articles per week
- Each Friday: add internal links from new articles to older related articles
- Each Sunday: check GSC impressions, CTR, and average position for all articles
- Update titles on any article ranking position 8–20 but with low CTR

### Weeks 9–16 — Authority and Monetisation
- Continue 2 articles per week
- At 25+ articles: submit Google AdSense application (already done — await approval)
- Apply for Zoho affiliate via CJ Affiliate once content referencing Zoho is live
- At 30+ articles: assess Impact.com reapplication readiness
- Review top 5 articles monthly for outdated pricing, limits, or features

### Ongoing — Compounding Growth
- Maintain publishing rhythm even when traffic is flat
- Prioritise cluster depth over breadth (finish a silo before branching wide)
- Treat month 3–6 as the hardest phase — traffic grows but income lags

## Realism Check — What to Expect

### What will very likely work
- **Long-tail SEO traffic will come.** "Free [tool] alternatives" keywords have low competition. New sites can rank for these within 3–5 months.
- **AdSense becomes realistic** once the site reaches roughly 200–300 daily visitors. With 50 well-structured articles, this is achievable by month 6–9.
- **Affiliate income starts small.** Expect $5–30/month from affiliates in the first few months. Tools with consistent daily usage (cloud storage, productivity, password managers) outperform ad-hoc tools.
- **Pinterest is underrated.** Articles in design and creative categories (Canva, fonts, stock photos) can attract consistent Pinterest referral traffic once images are pinned.
- **Cluster articles compound.** A silo with 8–9 well-linked articles will rank significantly better than 8 isolated pages across different topics.

### What will not work fast
- Google does not rank new sites quickly. Expect 3–6 months before any meaningful organic traffic appears.
- Social media alone will not drive enough traffic without SEO backing.
- High-commission affiliate programs (HubSpot, enterprise SaaS) require significant traffic before meaningful conversions.

### Realistic income timeline
| Period | Estimated monthly income |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Month 1–3 | $0–20 |
| Month 3–6 | $20–100 |
| Month 6–9 | $100–500 |
| Month 9–12 | $500–1,000+ |

These numbers assume consistent 2-articles-per-week publishing and no major site errors.

## Consistency Principle

The single most important thing from here is consistency.

**Two articles per week, every week, for 12 months.**

Sites that reach $500–$1,000/month are almost always the ones that simply did not quit between month 3 and month 6, when traffic is growing but income is still small. The compounding effect means month 12 output will be significantly higher than months 1–3, but only if the publishing rhythm holds through the flat period.

Steady publishing and incremental improvements every week outperform occasional large bursts with long gaps.
