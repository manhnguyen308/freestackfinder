## Quick verdict

Microsoft 365 Personal costs $99.99 per year. For the majority of users — students, home users, freelancers, and small businesses — the answer to "do I need to keep paying?" is probably not. **Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides** is the best free MS Office alternative for most people: it works on any device, handles real-time collaboration better than Office, and covers 90% of everyday document, spreadsheet, and presentation needs at zero cost. **LibreOffice** is the right free alternative if you work offline regularly or need the full power of a desktop office suite. **OnlyOffice** is the pick if .docx file fidelity is your top priority.

---

## Why switching away from Microsoft Office works for most users

Microsoft 365 was designed for enterprise and professional environments where advanced Excel features, Outlook integration, and SharePoint connectivity justify the cost. For individual users writing documents, building basic spreadsheets, and creating presentations, the majority of Microsoft 365's value sits in features that most users never open.

The free alternatives below have been tested against Microsoft 365 on five real-world tasks: writing a formatted report, building a spreadsheet with formulas, creating a presentation, collaborating in real-time, and opening a complex .docx file. The results are more capable than most people expect.

## The best free Microsoft Office alternatives in 2026

### 1. Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides — best free MS Office alternative for most people

**What it is:** Google's free productivity suite included with any Google account — a complete replacement for Word, Excel, and PowerPoint that works in any browser.

**Free plan includes:**
- Google Docs (Word equivalent) — unlimited documents with full formatting
- Google Sheets (Excel equivalent) — full formula support including XLOOKUP, pivot tables, and IMPORTRANGE
- Google Slides (PowerPoint equivalent) — transitions, animations, and presenter view
- Google Forms for surveys and data collection
- 15GB free storage on Google Drive (shared with Gmail and Photos)
- Real-time collaboration — multiple people editing the same document simultaneously
- Full version history with restore capability
- Works on any browser, iOS, and Android
- Import and export .docx, .xlsx, and .pptx files
- Offline mode available via Chrome extension

**What the free plan is missing:**
- Less powerful than Excel for advanced data analysis and macro-heavy workflows
- No desktop app — browser-only (though offline mode works well)
- 15GB storage is shared across Gmail, Drive, and Photos — can fill up for heavy users
- Complex .docx formatting may shift slightly on export back to Word

**Who it's best for:** Students, remote workers, small teams, and anyone who collaborates regularly or works across multiple devices. This is the right choice for the vast majority of home and office users.

**Why it stands out:** Google's free suite has quietly become better than Microsoft Office for collaboration. The real-time co-editing, commenting, and suggestion features are more seamless than Microsoft's equivalents. The Sheets formula engine now covers nearly everything Excel users need day-to-day. For team communication alongside Google Workspace, see our [free Slack alternatives guide](/productivity/slack-alternatives/) for the best free options to pair with it.

[Use Google Workspace free →](https://workspace.google.com)

### 2. LibreOffice — best free desktop Microsoft Office replacement

**What it is:** A free, open-source desktop office suite with Writer (Word), Calc (Excel), Impress (PowerPoint), Draw, Base (database), and Math. Runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux with no subscription.

**Free plan includes:**
- LibreOffice Writer — advanced word processor with styles, mail merge, and bibliography tools
- LibreOffice Calc — comprehensive spreadsheet with macro support and extensive formula library
- LibreOffice Impress — presentation software with transitions, animations, and slide master
- Opens and saves .docx, .xlsx, and .pptx files natively
- No internet connection required — full offline capability
- Available on Windows, macOS, and Linux
- No subscription, no ads, no premium tier

**What it is missing:**
- Interface looks dated compared to Microsoft Office or Google's suite
- .docx compatibility is good but not perfect — complex table formatting or specific fonts may shift
- Real-time collaboration requires third-party plugins or workarounds
- Less intuitive for users coming from Office's ribbon interface

**Who it's best for:** Home office users who work offline, Linux users, and anyone who needs the full power of a desktop office suite without a subscription or internet dependency.

**Why it stands out:** LibreOffice is the most powerful free desktop office suite available. The Calc formula engine, Writer's style system, and Impress's animation capabilities are all genuinely professional-grade. For users who need to work on complex documents, produce print-ready reports, or manage large spreadsheets without an internet connection, LibreOffice is the right free MS Office alternative. For organising notes and knowledge alongside your documents, see our [free Notion alternatives guide](/productivity/notion-alternatives/) for the best free knowledge management tools to pair with LibreOffice.

[Download LibreOffice free →](https://www.libreoffice.org)

### 3. OnlyOffice Desktop — best for .docx file fidelity

**What it is:** A cross-platform office suite built around the Office Open XML format (.docx, .xlsx, .pptx), designed to open and save Microsoft Office files with near-perfect fidelity.

**Free plan includes:**
- Word, Spreadsheet, and Presentation editors
- Excellent .docx, .xlsx, .pptx compatibility — best in class for format fidelity
- Clean, modern interface that feels familiar to Office users
- Available for Windows, macOS, and Linux

**What it is missing:**
- Less feature breadth than LibreOffice for advanced use cases
- Cloud collaboration features are limited on the free desktop version
- Smaller community and fewer third-party resources than LibreOffice

**Who it's best for:** Users who exchange files regularly with Microsoft Office users and need files to open and display correctly on both ends — particularly for client-facing documents or files sent to printers.

**Why it stands out:** OnlyOffice's differentiator is that it uses the same Open XML format as Microsoft Office natively, rather than converting between formats. This means a .docx file opened in OnlyOffice looks the same as in Word — tables, fonts, margins, and formatting all render with the highest fidelity of any free alternative. If your primary concern is "will this document look right when the client opens it in Word?", OnlyOffice is the answer.

[Try OnlyOffice Desktop free →](https://www.onlyoffice.com/download-desktop.aspx)

### 4. WPS Office free — capable but with privacy trade-offs

**What it is:** A Microsoft Office-compatible suite with a polished interface, developed by Chinese company Kingsoft. The free version includes ads.

**Free plan includes:**
- Word, Spreadsheet, and Presentation editors
- Good Office format compatibility
- 1GB free cloud storage
- Mobile apps for iOS and Android

**What it is missing:**
- Ads in the interface on the free tier
- WPS Office is developed by a Chinese company — worth considering for users with data privacy requirements
- Occasional prompts to upgrade

**Who it's best for:** Users who want a polished Office-like interface and are comfortable with the trade-offs of an ad-supported product from a non-Western developer.

**Why it stands out:** WPS Office Free is included here primarily for completeness — it is a capable free MS Office alternative, but the privacy considerations and ad-supported model make it a lower priority recommendation than Google Docs, LibreOffice, or OnlyOffice for most users.

[Download WPS Office free →](https://www.wps.com)

## When Microsoft 365 is actually worth keeping

Microsoft 365 remains the right choice when you are in a workplace where everyone uses Office and file compatibility must be perfect, when you use advanced Excel features like Power Query, Power Pivot, or VBA macros extensively, when you need 1TB of OneDrive storage (included with the subscription), or when Outlook's calendar and email management is central to your workflow.

For individual users who primarily write documents, build basic spreadsheets, and create occasional presentations, the free alternatives above cover the full use case.

## Our verdict

For most people asking "what is the best free alternative to Microsoft Office?", **Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides** is the correct answer — it is free, works on any device, and handles real-time collaboration better than Office. For users who need a desktop-first, offline-capable suite, **LibreOffice** is the most powerful free option. For users who exchange files with Office users and need formatting to transfer perfectly, **OnlyOffice** solves that specific problem better than any other free tool. Microsoft 365 is worth the annual fee for professional environments with specific requirements — but for most individual users, the free alternatives are more than sufficient.
